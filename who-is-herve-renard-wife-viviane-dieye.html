Who is Herve Renard wife, Viviane Diye?

Hervé Renard was born on September 30, 1968, in Aix-les-Bains, France. From an early age, he displayed a deep passion for football and dreamt of becoming a professional player. However, fate had different plans for him, leading him toward a successful career in coaching.

His coaching journey began in the early 1990s when he worked as an assistant coach at various French clubs, learning the intricacies of the game and honing his tactical acumen.

His big break came in 2003 when he was appointed as the head coach of the Vietnamese national team. Though his time in Vietnam was short-lived, he gained valuable international coaching experience.

It was in Africa that Renard truly made a name for himself as one of the continent’s most successful coaches. In 2008, he was appointed as the head coach of the Zambia national team.

He led the Zambian team to a remarkable triumph in the 2012 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) in Gabon and Equatorial Guinea, a victory that held immense significance due to the team’s history and the tragic plane crash in 1993 that took the lives of the Zambian national squad.

His impressive achievements with Zambia did not go unnoticed, and he was subsequently appointed as the head coach of the Ivory Coast national team in 2014. Under his guidance, the Ivory Coast team clinched the 2015 AFCON title in Equatorial Guinea, adding another major trophy to Renard’s growing list of accomplishments.

His success story continued when he became the head coach of the Moroccan national team in 2016. In the 2018 AFCON held in Egypt, he displayed his tactical prowess once again, leading the Moroccan team to the knockout stages of the competition.

His ability to motivate players, adapt tactics to various opponents, and foster team unity earned him widespread admiration from football fans and professionals around the world. Renard became renowned for his signature white shirt and tie look on the sidelines during matches.

Apart from his work with national teams, Renard also had successful stints with various football clubs. He coached clubs in Africa, including USM Alger in Algeria and TP Mazembe in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, guiding the latter to victory in the CAF Champions League.

Why Did Killaboi Killed His Girlfriend? Murder Case Update

Killaboi has confessed to the murder of his girlfriend, Augusta Osedion after he was arrested for internet fraud by EFCC.

Killaboi is a Nigerian social media influencer. 

His Instagram profile portrays him as a Blockchain Developer and Crypto Enthusiast. He doesn’t have many posts, but the few he has show him living a luxurious lifestyle.

In one of his posts, he poses behind a private jet and comments that his lifestyle might make people think he has some secret advantage. 

In 2021, he got into trouble when he was arrested by the (EFCC) Economic and Financial Crimes Commission for using a stolen card to carry out a significant transaction of N150 million through a point-of-sale (POS) machine. He was released on bail.

However, things took a darker turn in July 2023. Killaboi admitted to killing his girlfriend, Augusta Osedion. He claimed they had an argument and accidentally stabbed her during the altercation.

After the incident, he fled and has been struggling with feelings of wanting to end his own life.

Why Did Killaboi Killed His Girlfriend? Murder Case Update

Killaboi, known on social media, confessed that he accidentally stabbed his girlfriend during an argument.

He expressed remorse and said he would surrender to the police to face the consequences.

Her mother had been looking for her after she went missing and had tried to help them reconcile before.

In a series of updates on his Instagram story, he also shared screenshots of a message he sent to the girl’s mother, explaining that he didn’t intend to kill her.

Killaboi regretted what happened and shared emotional posts on Instastory about how his life was ruined at 26.

He promised to ensure his late girlfriend got justice and admitted to running away in fear after the incident. He also shared a message he sent to her mother.

According to witnesses, the police accessed Killaboi’s apartment through the fence and found his girlfriend’s body badly injured. There were allegations that some of her vital organs were missing. Her car was found at the scene as well.

On the other hand, according to the Lagos Police Command, the deceased’s mother reported to the police that her daughter had a quarrel with her boyfriend on July 11.

The mother tried to reconcile the two but couldn’t reach them on their phones or other platforms.

The mother led the police to the boyfriend’s house in Oral Estate, Ikota, Ajah, where they found the girl’s Mercedes Benz C30 but couldn’t access the apartment.

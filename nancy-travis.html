Nancy Travis is an American actor, producer, and voice artist who is known for having been cast as Sylvia Bennington in the comedy film Three Men and a Baby and Three Men and a Little Lady, Vanessa Baxter in the television sitcom Last Man Standing, Kim Cooper in the CBS sitcom series Almost Perfect, Julie Better in the television sitcom series Work with Me, Prof. Joyce Reardon in the mystery horror thriller miniseries Stephen King’s Rose Red, Christine “Chris” Connor in the CBS sitcom Becker, Joan Barry in the biographical comedy-drama film Chaplin, Susan Pearson in the TBS sitcom The Bill Engvall Show, Donna Hodges in the crime drama television series Mr. Mercedes, Corinne Landreaux in the action comedy film Air America, Lisa in the comedy-drama television series The Kominsky Method, Isabelle “Mama” McMurray in the neo-Western drama television series Ride, Cassie Slocombe in the comedy film Passed Away, and Kathleen “Kathy” Avilla in the crime thriller film Internal Affairs. In addition to that, her film credits also include Married to the Mob, I’ll Be Home for Christmas, Eight Men Out, How Much Is Really True?, Loose Cannons, The Vanishing, So I Married an Axe Murderer, Lieberman in Love, Fluke, Greedy, The Submarine Kid, Destiny Turns on the Radio, Bogus, My Last Love, The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants, Beyond Suspicion, The Jane Austen Book Club, Dissonance, Squatters, Bernard and Huey, and Night of the Animated Dead.

    Nancy Travis Facts

  • She grew up in Framingham, Massachusetts, and Baltimore, Maryland.
  • Nancy Travis made her first theatrical film appearance by playing the role of Sylvia Bennington (Mary’s mother who is an actor with whom Jack had a fling) in the 1987 comedy film Three Men and a Baby, starring Tom Selleck, Steve Guttenberg, and Ted Danson.
  • She portrayed the character of Joan Barry (an American woman who won a paternity suit in California in 1943 against Charlie Chaplin) in the 1992 biographical comedy-drama film Chaplin.
  • Along with Amber Tamblyn, America Ferrera, Blake Lively, Alexis Bledel, Bradley Whitford, Rachel Ticotin, and Jenna Boyd, Nancy Travis co-starred as Lydia Rodman (Carmen’s stepmother) in the comedy-drama film The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants in 2005.
  • From 2011 to 2021, she brought to life the character of Vanessa Baxter (née Davidson) (Mike’s wife who is a geologist working in the energy industry) in the television sitcom Last Man Standing. Travis’ co-stars for the project included Tim Allen, Kaitlyn Dever, Christoph Sanders, Amanda Fuller, and others.

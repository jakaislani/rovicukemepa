Trey Lyles Height: How Tall Is Trey Lyles?

Trey Lyles height-Trey Anthony Lyles is a Canadian professional basketball player who was born on November 5th, 1995 in Saskatoon, Canada.

Lyles was born to Thomas Lyles and Jasenka Lyles. It is unclear to us as to whether Lyles shares the same parents with any sibling of his as he is believed to be the only child of his parents.

He attended the Arsenal Technical High School and later furthered to the University of Kentucky in 2014 and graduated in 2015.

Trey Lyles career

Lyles was one of the top high school basketball players in the country going into his senior year of high school in Indianapolis, and Kentucky, Indiana, and Louisville all actively pursued him. When Lyles was a student in high school, in September 2010, he made a commitment to Indiana, but in August 2012, he revoked that commitment.

On November 5, 2013, Lyles signed a letter of intent to play football and attend the University of Kentucky. Lyles led Arsenal Tech to the IHSAA Class 4A State Championship game with a 63-59 victory over Lake Central during his senior year of high school, averaging 23.7 points, 12.9 rebounds, and 3.5 assists per game.

Following the conclusion of his senior year, Lyles received more votes for Indiana Mr. Basketball than James Blackmon Jr. and Trevon Bluiett, both of whom signed to play at Indiana University and Xavier University.

On June 12, 2014, Lyles signed up for college in Kentucky. Lyles missed Kentucky’s six-game exhibition series in Nassau, Bahamas from August 10 to August 17 because of an unidentified leg issue.

Lyles helped the Wildcats to the 2015 NCAA Final Four as a freshman and was selected to the All-SEC Freshman Team in 2015.

On April 9, 2015, Lyles made the decision to declare for the 2015 NBA Draft along with his Kentucky teammates Andrew Harrison, Aaron Harrison, Dakari Johnson, Devin Booker, Karl-Anthony Towns, and Willie Cauley-Stein.

The Utah Jazz selected Lyles with the 12th overall choice in the 2015 NBA Draft on June 25, 2015. On July 7, he entered into a rookie scale contract with the Jazz. Over the first two months of his rookie season, Lyles averaged 3.0 points and 3.6 rebounds per game.

In January, he started to increase his output with more consistent minutes, and on January 4 against the Houston Rockets, he scored in double figures for the first time with 13 points. He first had 10 rebounds on January 9 in a victory against the Miami Heat, and on January 14 in a loss to the Sacramento Kings, he had a season-high 19 points.

In place of the injured Nikola Miroti on the World Team roster, he was designated a Rising Stars Challenge competitor for the 2016 NBA All-Star Weekend on February 3. He had a career-high 22 points on April 10 in a victory over the Denver Nuggets of 100-84.

Lyles and Tyler Lydon’s draft rights were transferred to the Denver Nuggets on June 22, 2017, along with the draft rights to Donovan Mitchell. He had a season-high 18 points on December 2, 2017, in a 115-100 victory over the Los Angeles Lakers.

He had a career-high 25 points on December 10, 2017, in a 126-116 overtime defeat against the Indiana Pacers. He set a new career best on January 5, 2018, scoring 26 points in a 99-91 victory over the Utah Jazz.

Lyles joined the San Antonio Spurs on July 12, 2019. In a 125-117 loss against the Portland Trail Blazers on February 6, 2020, Lyles recorded a double-double, tallying a season-high 23 points, 10 rebounds, and two steals.

In a 114-113 victory over the Orlando Magic on February 29, 2020, Lyles finished with 20 points, nine rebounds, two assists, four steals, and one block.

In a 116-111 loss to the Indiana Pacers on March 2, 2020, Lyles scored himself 20 points once more in addition to adding three rebounds, two assists, and one steal. Lyles was said to have undergone an appendectomy on July 15, 2020, and he was going to miss the rest of the 2019–20 campaign.

Lyles inked a contract with the Detroit Pistons on August 6, 2021. Lyles and Josh Jackson were acquired by the Sacramento Kings on February 10, 2022, as part of a four-team transaction that also sent Marvin Bagley III to the Pistons.

How tall is Trey Lyles?

Lyles stands at a height of 2.06 m tall and weighs 106kg.
